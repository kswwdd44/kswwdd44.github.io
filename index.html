About Me

Hi! My name is Sunwook Kim, a fourth year Ph.D. student in the School of Computing at KAIST. I am working with Juho Kim as a member of KIXLAB (KAIST Interaction Lab).

My research focuses on generative model-based interaction techniques that capture human implicit knowledge and reasoning. I also have a huge interest in reusing the captured human knowledge or reasoning process through a LLM- or LVM-based agent.

You can find an overview about my research work on my UIST 2025 Doctoral Symposium paper.

Ongoing Research

I did a recent work that captures and structures the reasoning processes of professionals within creative workflows, feeds this information to a generative-model–based agent, and observes the resulting effects.

As a next step, I'm exploring:

  • how to design a creative agent that can plan, act, and make decisions grounded in professional's reasoning directly within a design tool, and
  • how to built a general agent framework that acts like a human in various tasks (e.g., design, web navigation, environment interaction) through skill learning and context-based action selection.
